Lattices by example 1

August 8, 2025

First of all - I’m not mathematician, but engineer who loves challenges;)
I’m going to explain how I came to this topic by solving some interesting problems.

Challenge 1#

$a\sqrt{2} + b\sqrt{3} + c\sqrt{5} = 640.53550337526478156252876765…$

Find coefficients a, b, c in $\Z$

This can be quickly solved by Brute-force, but my topic is Lattice this time. And I want to explain how I think about it from Lattice perspective. And we can also easy extend challenge to 20 coefficients which makes BF method effectively out.

Important thing is that square root of prime is irrational number (cannot be expressed as fraction of two integers).
So each coefficient is multiplied by irrational number and result is also irrational.

If we want to solve similar challenge, but with rational numbers, say:

$a\frac{2}{3} + b\frac{3}{4} + c \frac{5}{6} = 8$.

we quickly find that there is infinite number of solution in $\Z$ ring. For any b, c we have corresponding a. And it holds for any rational numbers (fractions).

$\frac{8a + 9b + 10c}{12} = 8$.

$a = \frac{96 - 9b - 10c}{8}$

The same thing will happen if we round irrational numbers to some number of decimal digits, just coefficients will be pretty large depending od precision of rounding.

So back our to irrational numbers which assure unique solution. We are trying to find coefficients a, b, c such as left side of given equation is as much as possible close to irrational number on right side.

And exactly this bolded expression brings Lattice topic to the scene:

Lattice#

There is a lot of articles and videos about Lattices, basis vectors and finding shortest (SVP) or closest(CVP) vector. I do not want to repeat it, but we will need some brief intro for context.
I point to the most important things, how I understand them and how they are related to my challenge.

  • I will talk exclusively about Lattices over Integers ($\Z$), Such Lattice is exactly what its names suggest - group of points in space with integer coordinates
  • Important parameter of each Lattice is its dimension. Thus 2 or 3 dimensional Lattice is easy to imagine as some periodical structure in 2 or 3 dimensions. When I started with this topic, I tried to extend this image to higher dimensions, but it is hard. This is reason why my first challenge is with 3 coefficients and it really works also in higher dimensions.
